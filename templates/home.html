About Us
Krishi Nepal

Agriculture is a very important sector for Nepal, as it employs about 66% of the population and contributes about 33% of the GDP1. Nepal has a diverse geography, climate, and culture, which affect the types of crops and livestock that can be grown and raised. Some of the main agricultural products of Nepal are rice, wheat, maize, millet, barley, sugarcane, oilseeds, fruits, vegetables, spices, tea, coffee, tobacco, jute, and dairy products. Nepal also has a rich tradition of organic farming and agroforestry.

Agriculture also plays an important role in Nepal's cultural and social life, with many festivals and traditions centered around the planting and harvesting of crops. Overall, agriculture is a vital component of Nepal's economy and society, and will continue to be so for the foreseeable future.
